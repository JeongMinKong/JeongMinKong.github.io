Jeong Min Kong

I am a 1st year graduate student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Toronto, working with Professor Elvino Sousa as a member of the Bell University Lab. I completed my BASc in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the same university in 2022.

My research interests broadly span machine learning, information theory, and wireless communications. My current work involves designing optimal 3D trajectories of multi-purpose UAVs using reinforcement learning.

I have been extremely fortunate to have worked under brilliant scholars & mentors in my undergrad, including Professor Adrian Nachman and Professor Raviraj Adve at the University of Toronto, Professor Merouane Debbah and Professor George Alexandropoulos at Huawei Research, Professor Kathrin Wimmer at The University of Tokyo, and Professor Ekram Hossain at the University of Manitoba.
